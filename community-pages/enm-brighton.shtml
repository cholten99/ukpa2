What it's like

ENM Brighton is newcomer-friendly, discussion-focused, warm and non-judgmental — and deliberately de-sexualised. Events range from book clubs and crafting afternoons to casual pub nights, all centred on open, supportive conversation rather than dating.

It's a community built for people who want to talk honestly about ENM — sharing vulnerabilities, learning from each other, and finding connection. Romantic connections do of course evolve naturally, but this isn't a cruising or find-a-date environment.

Brief history

ENM Brighton was founded in 2022 by non-binary and queer organisers who wanted to create a space for open, vulnerable conversations among ENM folks — not just dating. It has since grown to a community of around 200 people, running regular in-person events in central Brighton.

How to join

Follow @enmbrighton on Instagram for event announcements, or visit enmbrighton.com. You can also reach out by email. There is no WhatsApp group — this is intentional.

Privacy

  • No photos without consent.
  • The member list is not shared outside the group.
  • No WhatsApp presence — a deliberate choice by the organisers.

Accessibility

Events move between two venues in central Brighton:

  • The Queery – wheelchair-accessible entrance, seating and toilet; gender-neutral toilets; sober space. A small, intimate venue — worth noting it can feel cosy at capacity, and noise can sometimes be a challenge.
  • The Actors pub – a relaxed pub setting in central Brighton.
